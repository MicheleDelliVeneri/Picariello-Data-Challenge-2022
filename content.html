Eustema Data Challenge

This is the official page of the Eustema Data Challenge organized by University of Naples Federico II in collaboration with Eustema. If you are interested in the structure of the Challenge continue reading this page, if you want to go streight to the data, look here

Registration to the Challenge

The first step to compete in the challenge is to register your group. To do so send a mail to michele.delliveneri@na.infn.it containing the following information:

  • Names of the partecipants
  • Department, name and email of your supervisor

Please keep in mind that groups should contain a maximum number of 3 students which may or may not be from the same department.

Objective and Structure of the Challenge

The objective of the challenge is to simulate an industrial Data Science project as you would encounter working as a data scientist for a company or an interdisciplinary research group. The challenge is about predicting the outcome, the duration in number of days and the settlement for around 300,000 lowsuits produced by several tribunals of the "Giudice di Pace" scattered around Italy. The groups will be provided at the start of the challenge with a subset of the raw data containing the target variables and, later on in the challenge, you will be asked to predict those target variables for a "blind" test set. Given that the raw data are "probably" not suited to directly be used to make predictions due to all kinds of data problems, and given that we are simulating a realistic data scientist workflow, the challenge is splitted into two phases:

  • Data Discovery and Exploration: you should analyze the data in order to search for correlations, missing data, outliers and so on. At the deadline you will need to provide us a report about your findings and also the code that was used to analyze the data. Each group will be evaluated on the basis of the quality and completeness of their analysis, the clarity with wich they findings are explaining and the quality of their code. The point is not only making a correct and complete analysis but also convincing us to trust your analysis.
  • Target Parameter Estimation: each group will be provided with a common train and validation set and ask to make predictions on the three target variables on a blind test set. You may decide to approach this three problems but your scores will be based on the following metrics:
    F1-Score for the outcome classification problem;
    INSERIRE METRICA duration;
    INSERIRE METRICA settlement.

Data Fields

The following data fields are available in the data_exploration.csv file. A brief description of all the fields is as follows:

  • ID: an unique identifier to each row of the dataset;
  • Judge: the judge that handles the lawsuit;
  • Giudge Identifier: an unique ID given to each Judge for each lowsuit;
  • Date of the Identifier: the date in which the Giudge Identifier was assigned;
  • Section: the section of the Judge;
  • Date of the first Court Hearing: date of the firtst court hearing;
  • Value: the monetary value of the lowsuit;
  • Unified Contribution: is the cost to start the lowsuit;
  • Last Communication from the Office: the date of the last communication from the judge office;
  • Office Code: the unique identifier of the Judge Office;
  • Year: the year in which the lowsuit took place;
  • Copies Request: a boolean flag indicating if a request of the lowsuit document copies was forwarded to the Judge Office
  • Actor: the actor of the lowsuit, i.e. the accusing part;
  • Defendant: the principal defendant of the lowsuit;
  • Number of Lowyers: the number of lowyers involved in the lowsuit;
  • Number of Legal Parties: the number of legal parties involved in the lowsuit;
  • Number of Legal Entities: the number of legal entities involved in the lowsuit;
  • Number of Persons: the number of persons involved in the lowsuit;
  • City of the Judge Office: the city in which the Judge Office is located;
  • Date of the lowsuit: the date when the loswuit started;
  • Duration XXXXX
  • Outcome: the outcome of the lowsuit (the first target variable);
  • Duration: the duration of the lowsuit in number of days (the second target variable)
  • Settlement: the final settlement of the lowsuit (the third target variable);
