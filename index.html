The Challenge

The objective of the challenge is to simulate an industrial Data Science project as you would encounter working as a data scientist for a company or an interdisciplinary research group. The challenge is about predicting the outcome, the duration in number of days and the settlement for around 300,000 lawsuits produced by several tribunals of the "Giudice di Pace" scattered around Italy.
In order to register to the challenge and to see the timeline take a look at Registration and Timeline
To know more about the data, take a look at Data
To know more about the scoring, take a look at Scoring
To know how to submit your results, take a look at Submission

At the beginning of the challenge, each group will be provided with training set containing all the input data fields and the values for the target variables and a blind test set for which you will have to make predictions. Given that we are simulating an industrial data science project, we are not only interested about the performances of your algorithms on the Test set, but also on the completeness and correctness of your Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and the quality of both your code and report explaining your strategy to solve the problems and the details of the algorithms and method used.

The Data

The data will be provided on Microsoft Teams at the beginning of the challenge and will be consituted by 2 .csv files:

  • Training Set: containing all the data fields and the 3 target variables
  • Blind Test Set: containing all the data fields but no target variables

The following data fields are present:

  • ID: an unique identifier to each row of the dataset
  • Judge: the judge that handles the lawsuit
  • Giudge Identifier: an unique ID given to each Judge for each lowsuit
  • Date of the Identifier: the date in which the Giudge Identifier was assigned
  • Section: the section of the Judge
  • Date of the first Court Hearing: date of the firtst court hearing
  • Value: the monetary value of the lowsuit
  • Unified Contribution: is the cost to start the lowsuit;
  • Last Communication from the Office: the date of the last communication from the judge office
  • Office Code: the unique identifier of the Judge Office
  • Year: the year in which the lowsuit took place
  • Copies Request: a boolean flag indicating if a request of the lowsuit ocument copies was forwarded to the Judge Office
  • Actor: the actor of the lowsuit, i.e. the accusing part
  • Defendant: the principal defendant of the lowsuit
  • Number of Lowyers: the number of lowyers involved in the lowsuit
  • Number of Legal Parties: the number of legal parties involved in the lowsuit
  • Number of Legal Entities: the number of legal entities involved in the lowsuit
  • Number of Persons: the number of persons involved in the lowsuit
  • City of the Judge Office: the city in which the Judge Office is located
  • Date of the lowsuit: the date when the loswuit started
  • Duration XXXXX
  • Outcome: the outcome of the lowsuit (the first target variable) which can be on of the following classes:
    • 0 Accepted
    • 1 Declined
    • 2 Partially Accepted
    • 3 Ceased Matter
  • Duration: the duration of the lowsuit in number of days (the second target variable)
  • Settlement: the final settlement of the lowsuit (the third target variable)


Scoring

The scoring of the challenge is based on a point system in which the minimum score is 0 and the maximum is 100. The total number of points is divided among the three taks of the challenge as follows:

  • up to 60 points are awarded on the basis of your metrics on the blind test set
  • up to 20 points are awarded on the basis of the quality of your report
  • up to 20 point are awarded on the basis of the quality of your code

Metrics

The following metrics will be used to evaluate your predictions:

  • Outcome classification metric: micro-averaged F1 Score. 20 points will be awarded for an F1 Score of 1.
  • Duration regression metric: Mean Absolute Eror (MAE). 20 points will be awarded for a MAE of 0.
  • Settlement regression metric: Mean Absolute Eror (MAE). 20 points will be awarded for a MAE of 0.

Finalists presentation scoring

The presentations of the finalist groups will be judged by a commission that will assign between 0 and 20 points on the basis of the ability of the groups to convince and sell their solution.

Submission

Soon after your group registration, and before the challenge begins a folder accessible only to the members of your group will be created in the Files folder of the Challenge Team. In order to complete the challenge your group will have to upload the following files before the 16th of September at midnight:

  1. A file called prediction.csv containing, for all the instances in the Test set, your predictions for the outcome (int), the duration (int) and the settlement (float32). The file must have a header with 4 columns named respectively ID, Outcome, Duration, Settlement. You must provide the predictions for all the Test instances, otherwise your score will be invalidated
  2. A file called report.pdf outlining your EDA, the architectural choiches and an outline of your approach to solve the three problems;
  3. All the scripts used to solve the problems. The scripts can be written in the Python or R languages and they must reproduce your results. We adivise to follow an object oriented programming parading and to take a look at Python etiquette and R etiquette to make sure that your code is correctly commented and formatted.
